Lumma Stealer Analysis:

Based on the currently available data, Lumma Stealer is an information stealer malware that has been available through a malware-as-a-service (MaaS) model on Russian-speaking forums since at least August 2022. The malware primarily targets cryptocurrency wallets and two-factor authentication (2FA) browser extensions before stealing sensitive information from the victim's machine. Threat actor "Shamel" is believed to be the developer of Lumma Stealer, who goes by the alias "Lumma". The malware features a non-resident loader that can deliver additional payloads via EXE, DLL, and PowerShell files. A notable TTP ( Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures) mentioned in the data is the use of HTTP POST requests with the user agent "teslabrowser/5.5" to exfiltrate stolen data to a C2 server. The malware's use of MaaS platforms and its focus on cryptocurrency wallets and 2FA extensions suggest that it may be used by threat actors targeting financial institutions or individuals in the cryptocurrency space. Overall, Lumma Stealer appears to be a sophisticated information stealer malware with ties to Russian-speaking cybercrime groups.
