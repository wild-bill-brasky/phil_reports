Executive Summary

This report provides an overview of the current cyber threat landscape, highlighting key incidents, trends, and threat actors. Our analysis is based on a comprehensive review of publicly available OSINT data, including news articles, social media, and online forums.

Key Incidents

Microsoft Blames Latent Code Issue After Windows 11 Upgrades Sneak Past Admin Blockades

A recent incident reported by Microsoft highlights the vulnerability of Windows 11 to latent code issues. According to the company, Windows 11 upgrades have been sneaking past admin blockades, resulting in free blue screens of death for users. Microsoft has responded by rewarding those who patch early with bricks hurled through its operating system.

The recent incident involving Godaddy taking Zoom offline for about 90 minutes is another notable example of the impact of cyber attacks on critical infrastructure. The incident occurred when a manager of the US namespace attempted to block ZoomUS networks, causing the platform to become unavailable.

Another incident reported by Bleeping Computer highlights the ongoing threat posed by SonicWall Secure Mobile Access SMA appliances. A remote code execution vulnerability affecting these appliances has been under active exploitation since January 2025, with Chinese-speaking IronHusky hackers targeting Russian and Mongolian government organizations.

The recent phishing campaign launched by Mustong Panda, a China-aligned state-sponsored threat actor, has also been reported by Talos Intelligence. The campaign targeted an unspecified organization in Myanmar, using updated versions of a known backdoor called Toneshell.

Finally, the recent incident involving Tesla's Model Y owner claiming that Elon Musk had taken thousands of miles out of their odometer is another example of the potential for cyber attacks to cause significant damage to individuals and organizations.

Malware Roundup: 5 Most Active Malware in the Wild

Lumma Stealer Analysis:

Based on the currently available data, Lumma Stealer is an information stealer malware that has been available through a malware-as-a-service (MaaS) model on Russian-speaking forums since at least August 2022. The malware primarily targets cryptocurrency wallets and two-factor authentication (2FA) browser extensions before stealing sensitive information from the victim's machine. Threat actor "Shamel" is believed to be the developer of Lumma Stealer, who goes by the alias "Lumma". TTPs mentioned include a non-resident loader capable of delivering additional payloads via EXE, DLL, and PowerShell files. The malware uses HTTP POST requests with the user agent "TeslaBrowser/5.5" to exfiltrate stolen data to a C2 server. Lumma Stealer is notable for its use of a MaaS model, which allows threat actors to easily purchase and distribute the malware. Overall, Lumma Stealer represents a significant threat to cryptocurrency users and organizations with 2FA protections in place.
